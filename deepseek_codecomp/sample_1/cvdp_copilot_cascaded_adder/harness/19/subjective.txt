The configuration REG = 8'b11111110 offers better performance because it allows the last stage of the adder to function as a combinational element, eliminating the register delay inherent in the last stage of REG = 8'b11111111. While the first seven stages remain registered, introducing some delay, the elimination of the register in the final stage ensures that once the penultimate stage produces an intermediate result, the output can be generated without further latency. This results in a shorter overall pipeline depth, allowing more pipeline stages to be filled, thereby reducing the total time to produce a valid output.
