The design is not suitable for correcting multi-bit errors because Hamming codes primarily detect and correct single-bit errors. In the provided example, when two bits are flipped, the error syndrome calculated by the hamming_code_receiver module points to a single bit (bit1) for correction, which is incorrect. As a result, the module fails to properly correct the two-bit error, leading to potential data corruption. The design relies on the parity bits to identify discrepancies, but it cannot reliably distinguish between single and multiple errors, making it ineffective for correcting multi-bit errors.
