The throughput achieved by the matrix_multiplier module is approximately 95%. The design achieves this high throughput by implementing a pipelined architecture. Intermediate results such as partial products and partial sums are stored in registers and reused in subsequent clock cycles, allowing the computation to continue without significant delays. This pipelining technique ensures that each stage of the computation operates continuously, maximizing the utilization of the hardware resources and reducing the overall latency. Additionally, the design efficiently manages memory bandwidth by storing intermediate results locally rather than relying solely on external memory access, further enhancing throughput.
