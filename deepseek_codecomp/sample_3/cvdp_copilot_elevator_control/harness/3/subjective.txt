In the elevator control system's FSM, when transitioning from IDLE, the direction is determined by comparing the current floor with the max_request and min_request. If max_request > current_floor_reg, the elevator moves UP; otherwise, it moves DOWN. This ensures that the elevator prioritizes upward or downward requests as needed. The FSM avoids unnecessary direction changes by waiting until the elevator reaches the highest or lowest requested floor before halting. This approach ensures efficient handling of requests while maintaining minimal power consumption by keeping the elevator idle unless actively required to move.
