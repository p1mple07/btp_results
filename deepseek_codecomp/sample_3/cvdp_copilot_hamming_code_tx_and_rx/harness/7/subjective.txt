The design calculates a 3-bit syndrome (c3, c2, c1) to detect errors. If exactly one bit is flipped, the syndrome points directly to the incorrect bit, allowing correction. However, if two or more bits are flipped simultaneously, the syndrome may not uniquely identify the individual faulty bits. For instance, flipping two adjacent bits could result in a syndrome that matches a single-bit error pattern, leading to incorrect correction. Thus, the design cannot reliably correct multi-bit errors.
