The `reg_gpio_dir` and `module_active` signals work together to ensure that the module respects its own power-down state and correctly drives the GPIO pins. During power-down mode, `module_active` is inactive, causing `reg_gpio_dir` to have no effect on the GPIOs, thus maintaining them in a high-impedance state. This prevents metastability and ensures reliable operation. However, the direct assignment of `reg_gpio_dir` without considering `module_active` may lead to unintended behavior if changes occur while the module is powered down. Additionally, since `reg_gpio_dir` is initialized externally, manual updates are required, which could introduce errors if not handled carefully.
