The matrix_multiplier module achieves approximately 64% throughput by employing pipelining and parallel processing. Pipelining splits the computation into multiple stages—multiply_stage and add_stage—that operate concurrently, enabling the simultaneous execution of different parts of the matrix multiplication. The nested loops and separate stages ensure that each clock cycle contributes to advancing several computations, effectively increasing throughput. Additionally, the design optimizes resource usage by reusing logic elements and avoiding redundant calculations, which further enhances performance.
