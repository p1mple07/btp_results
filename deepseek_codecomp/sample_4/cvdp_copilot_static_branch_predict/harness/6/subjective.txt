The static_branch_predict module determines whether a branch is taken by examining the sign bit of the immediate offset stored in branch_imm. When handling certain instructions like jal, jalr, cb, and cj, the sign bit indicates whether the immediate represents a negative (branch likely missed) or positive (branch likely taken) offset. This approach simplifies the prediction process by focusing on a single bit, making implementation easier. However, relying solely on the sign bit can reduce accuracy, especially for instructions where the immediate's actual value isn't strictly positive or negative, potentially leading to missed branches or false predictions.
