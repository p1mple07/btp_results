The testbench uses a single clock signal to ensure that all three DUTs are triggered synchronously, eliminating asynchronous events. By synchronizing the reset, enable, and data signals to the same clock edges, the testbench guarantees that all operations occur at precisely defined intervals. The use of @(posedge clk) events in the monitoring process ensures that changes to the out_val and done signals are observed only at clock transitions, reducing the risk of race conditions. Although each DUT is configured with unique parameters, they all share the same clock and reset signals, which aligns their internal processes and timing. The run_test task uniformly resets the cycle counter and applies the reset and enable signals for each test sequence, ensuring a synchronized start for every test. Overall, this centralized timing control prevents timing mismatches and ensures consistent monitoring of the out_val and done signals across all instances.
