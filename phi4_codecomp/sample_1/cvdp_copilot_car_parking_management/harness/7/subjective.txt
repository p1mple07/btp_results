The simulate_entry task holds the vehicle_entry_sensor signal high for 10 ns to mimic a brief activation event that coincides with one clock period. This duration is chosen because the clock period in the simulation is 10 ns, ensuring that the sensor event is sampled at the correct time. The state transitions in the design occur on the clock edges, so a 10 ns pulse is sufficient to trigger the intended change. Changing the duration could affect the simulation by either missing the sensor event if the pulse is too short or causing unintended multiple detections if it is too long. A shorter pulse might not be registered by the FSM, while a longer pulse could lead to multiple state transitions within a single clock cycle. Therefore, maintaining the 10 ns duration is critical for accurately simulating the sensor's behavior and ensuring proper timing of state transitions.
