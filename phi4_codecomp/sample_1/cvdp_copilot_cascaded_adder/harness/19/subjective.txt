The first stage of the cascaded adder is inherently combinational and is optimized to operate quickly, so leaving it combinational avoids the extra clock cycle delay that a register would introduce. Registering only the later stages (as in REG = 8'b11111110) effectively breaks up the critical path while keeping the initial stage fast. In contrast, adding a register in the first stage (REG = 8'b11111111) unnecessarily increases the overall latency by introducing an extra clock cycle delay at the very start of the adder.
