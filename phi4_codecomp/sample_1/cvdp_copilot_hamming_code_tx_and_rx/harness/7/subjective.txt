The Hamming code receiver module computes parity bits to form a syndrome that indicates the bit position of a single error, flagging an error when the syndrome is nonzero. In the case of two flipped bits, the syndrome becomes the sum of the two error positions, resulting in an incorrect index for correction. Consequently, while the module correctly detects an error and attempts to flip the bit at position 6 for the modified data '10011110', it fails to correct the second error, demonstrating that the design is only capable of correcting single-bit errors.
