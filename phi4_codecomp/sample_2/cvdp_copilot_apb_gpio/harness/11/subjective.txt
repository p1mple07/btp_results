The reg_gpio_dir signal configures each GPIO pin as an output when asserted, while the module_active signal, derived from the negation of the power-down register, ensures that outputs are only driven when the module is active. During reset conditions, all registers including reg_gpio_dir are initialized to zero, which forces the pins to be in input mode and remain tri-stated, ensuring a known state upon startup. When power-down is enabled (reg_power_down = 1), module_active becomes 0, causing even pins configured as outputs to be tri-stated and preventing unintended drive. However, potential limitations include transient glitches during transitions between active and power-down states and the possibility that input signals may not be fully isolated when power-down is asserted, which could lead to unexpected behavior if the control signals are not properly synchronized.
