In the barrel shifter, the mask signal complicates validation for mode 11 because it selectively filters the shifted result, making the expected output dependent on both the shifting operation and the specific bits set in the mask. The testbench verifies its correctness by applying a known input, a predefined mask value, and then comparing the resulting output with the expected value using simulation delays, display messages, and conditional checks. This validation is critical because ensuring the mask functions correctly is essential for maintaining data integrity and preventing propagation of erroneous bits in downstream logic.
