The signal z1 is generated by ANDing the most significant bit (MSB) and the second MSB of binary_sum, indicating that the binary sum falls within the range of 12 to 15 (i.e., values 0xC to 0xF), which is invalid in BCD representation. The signal z2 is produced by ANDing the MSB with the third bit of binary_sum, detecting sums in the range of 10 to 11 (i.e., values 0xA and 0xB), which are also outside the valid BCD range. The binary_cout signal, which is the carry-out from the four-bit adder, flags cases where the binary sum exceeds 9 (i.e., values 10 and above), ensuring that any result outside the valid BCD range is correctly identified.
