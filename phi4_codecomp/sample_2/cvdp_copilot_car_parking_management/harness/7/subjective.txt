The simulate_entry task holds the vehicle_entry_sensor signal high for 10ns because this duration corresponds to one full clock cycle, ensuring that the signal is active long enough for the DUT to reliably detect the event. The clock period in the testbench is 10ns, so maintaining the sensor high for this period guarantees that the rising edge of the clock captures the sensor activation. This timing is critical for the state machine in the DUT to transition from the IDLE state to the ENTRY_PROCESSING state as designed. If the duration were shortened, the sensor signal might not be sampled properly, potentially causing the DUT to miss the entry event and remain in an incorrect state. Conversely, if the duration were extended beyond one clock cycle, it could trigger multiple or delayed state transitions, adversely affecting the simulation’s accuracy. Therefore, the 10ns duration is deliberately chosen to align with the clock period and ensure that state transitions occur at the intended times.
