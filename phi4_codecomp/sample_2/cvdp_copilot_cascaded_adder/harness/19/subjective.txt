The first stage in a cascaded adder typically performs a simple operation with minimal delay, so leaving it combinational avoids the extra clock cycle delay introduced by a register. Registering every stage, as in REG = 8'b11111111, unnecessarily adds a clock cycle to the overall latency even though the first stage's combinational delay is already low. Consequently, using REG = 8'b11111110 reduces the total time to produce a valid output by pipelining only the stages that benefit from registration while keeping the fast, minimal delay of the first stage.
