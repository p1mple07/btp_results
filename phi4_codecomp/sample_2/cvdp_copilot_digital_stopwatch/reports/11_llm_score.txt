In the dig_stopwatch module, if the start_stop signal pauses the stopwatch midway through counting to the next second, the internal counter stops incrementing and retains its current value. one_sec_pulse remains low because it asserts high only when the counter reaches COUNTER_MAX. This preserves the mid-count state, effectively freezing the seconds, minutes, and hour counters at their current values until start_stop toggles again to resume counting. When the stopwatch restarts, it resumes from the paused count, completing the remainder of the time unit before advancing to the next second.


Response:
When the start_stop signal goes low, the clock divider always block stops incrementing the counter and does not generate the one_sec_pulse signal, even if the counter had reached its maximum value. As a result, the one_sec_pulse remains deasserted, and the stopwatch logic triggered by its rising edge does not execute its counting operations. Consequently, the seconds, minutes, and hour counters remain unchanged, effectively pausing the timekeeping. Once the start_stop signal is reasserted, the counter resumes incrementing and a one_sec_pulse is generated, allowing the stopwatch to continue counting.
