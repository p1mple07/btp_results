In the IDLE state, the FSM evaluates the highest (max_request) and lowest (min_request) active floor requests relative to the current floor. It then compares the current floor with these values, transitioning to MOVING_UP if max_request is above the current floor and to MOVING_DOWN if min_request is below the current floor. This approach ensures that the elevator always moves toward the nearest pending request, optimizing travel time and efficiency. By committing to a single direction based on the relative positions of the requests, the logic prevents unnecessary direction changes and maintains a consistent, efficient service pattern.
