The elevator control system uses call_requests_internal to maintain an internal record of active floor requests, ensuring that the system always has an up-to-date set of calls. It integrates new external requests by performing a bitwise OR between call_requests_internal and the external call_requests input, thereby adding any new requests to the internal register. The system clears a serviced request by resetting the corresponding bit in call_requests_internal when the elevator reaches that floor, which prevents the elevator from stopping at the same floor twice. This mechanism prevents duplicate stops by ensuring that once a request is cleared, it is no longer considered active. During movement, the continuous update of call_requests_internal allows the elevator to dynamically adjust its direction based on the highest or lowest pending request, optimizing its route. In idle state, the internal register ensures that only unfulfilled requests trigger a state change, leading to efficient and accurate floor servicing.
