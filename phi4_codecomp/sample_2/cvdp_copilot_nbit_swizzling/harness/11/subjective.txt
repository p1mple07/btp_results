If DATA_WIDTH is not divisible by 8, then the reversal loops in the 2'b11 swizzling mode will only process the first 8*(DATA_WIDTH/8) bits, leaving the remaining bits unassigned. As a result, expected_data_out will only reflect the reversed segments for the computed portion, while the leftover bits will remain at their default (typically zero or undefined) values. This incomplete reversal can cause a mismatch between expected_data_out and the actual data_out generated by the DUT, leading to verification errors.
