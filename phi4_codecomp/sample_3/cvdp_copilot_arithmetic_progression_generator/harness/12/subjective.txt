The testbench employs a single, globally shared clock signal that toggles at a fixed 10 ns period, ensuring that all DUT instances operate synchronously. By using one clock for all instances, the testbench guarantees that the sampling of out_val and done signals occurs simultaneously for each DUT, which minimizes the risk of race conditions. The controlled application of the reset and enable signals, with precise timing intervals, ensures that every instance is synchronized in starting and stopping its sequence generation. The design monitors the signals at the rising edge of the clock, a standard practice to avoid asynchronous events and timing mismatches. This unified clocking strategy ensures that even though each DUT has unique configurations, their internal operations are aligned in time. Consequently, the testbench effectively prevents race conditions and timing mismatches across multiple DUT instances by enforcing strict synchronization and controlled signal transitions.
