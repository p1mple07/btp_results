When the start_stop signal is deasserted (low) while the stopwatch is counting, the always block responsible for the clock divider stops incrementing the counter because the if (start_stop) condition fails. As a result, the counter remains at its current value, and the one_sec_pulse signal is cleared, preventing the generation of a one-second pulse. Without the one_sec_pulse event, the stopwatch logic that increments seconds, minutes, and hours is not triggered, effectively pausing the counting process. Once the start_stop signal is reasserted, the counter resumes incrementing until it reaches COUNTER_MAX, at which point a one-second pulse is generated and the counters begin to update again.
