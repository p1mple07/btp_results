In the IDLE state, the FSM scans the call_requests_internal array to compute the highest (max_request) and lowest (min_request) active floor requests. If max_request is greater than the current floor, the FSM transitions to MOVING_UP; otherwise, if min_request is less than the current floor, it transitions to MOVING_DOWN. This approach ensures that the elevator moves in the direction that minimizes travel distance, addressing the nearest request efficiently. By strictly following the if-else logic, the FSM avoids unnecessary direction changes, maintaining a consistent movement until the request is fully served.
