The system integrates new floor requests by OR-ing the external call_requests input with the internal register call_requests_internal, ensuring that any new request is added to the active list. When the elevator reaches a floor, the corresponding bit in call_requests_internal is cleared, indicating that the request has been served and preventing the elevator from stopping again at the same floor. This clearing mechanism is crucial in preventing duplicate stops by removing serviced requests from the active set, which streamlines the elevator's movement sequence. By continuously updating call_requests_internal with new requests while clearing served ones, the system maintains an accurate and up-to-date representation of pending floor calls. During idle states, the internal register is used to determine the highest and lowest pending requests, allowing the elevator to efficiently decide its next direction. In movement states, the mechanism ensures efficient handling by triggering door operations only when a fresh request is detected, thus avoiding unnecessary stops and optimizing the overall control flow.
