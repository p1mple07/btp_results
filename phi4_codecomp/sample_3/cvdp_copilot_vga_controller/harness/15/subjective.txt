The testbench simulates the VGA controller by generating a 25 MHz clock and incrementing counters to track the horizontal and vertical phases, comparing them against the expected durations (640 active pixels, 16 front porch, 96 sync pulse, and 48 back porch for horizontal, and 480 active lines, 10 front porch, 2 sync pulse, and 33 back porch for vertical) as defined by the 640 x 480 VGA specification. It prints diagnostic messages at the transition points between these phases, thereby verifying that the hsync signal transitions (from HIGH to LOW during the sync pulse and back to HIGH during the back porch) occur exactly when they should. Similarly, the vertical synchronization is confirmed by monitoring the line counter and printing messages when the vertical phases (active region, front porch, sync pulse, and back porch) begin, ensuring that the vsync signal conforms to the VGA timing standard.
