The testbench uses a single clock signal (clk) to synchronize all DUTs, ensuring that every operation, including sampling of the out_val and done signals, occurs in lockstep with the clock edges. Because all three DUT instances share the same clock, signal transitions and state changes occur simultaneously across the different modules, which helps prevent race conditions. The common reset and enable signals ensure that all instances are initialized and activated at the same time, thereby reducing the risk of timing mismatches. Each run_test task is designed to wait for the positive edge of the clock before reading or asserting the outputs, ensuring that the monitored signals are stable and correctly captured. The inclusion of a cycle counter and synchronized logging further aids in verifying that each DUT’s output is sampled at the correct clock cycle. Together, these strategies guarantee that the testbench can accurately monitor and validate the out_val and done signals across multiple instances without falling prey to race conditions or timing mismatches.
