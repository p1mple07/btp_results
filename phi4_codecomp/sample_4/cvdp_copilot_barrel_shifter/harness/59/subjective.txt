The mask signal complicates validation in mode 11 because it alters the shifted data by selectively preserving bits through a bitwise AND, making the expected output dependent on both the shift amount and the mask pattern. The testbench verifies the functionality by initializing known inputs, applying a specific mask value, executing the shift operation, and then comparing the actual output to a precomputed expected result. This validation is critical to ensure that the barrel shifter correctly implements the masked shift behavior, which is essential for applications requiring precise control over which bits are retained after shifting.
