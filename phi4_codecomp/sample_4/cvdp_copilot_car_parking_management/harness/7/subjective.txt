The simulate_entry task holds the vehicle_entry_sensor signal high for 10ns to mimic the brief moment when a vehicle is detected entering the parking system. This duration is deliberately chosen so that the input signal is stable long enough for the design's clock and state machine to register the event. In the simulation, the 10ns pulse corresponds to one clock cycle of the 100MHz clock, ensuring proper synchronization with the state transitions. Changing the pulse duration could disrupt the timing of the state machine; a shorter pulse might be too brief for detection, while a longer pulse could inadvertently trigger additional state transitions. The timing of the pulse is critical because the design's state transitions are triggered on the rising edge of the clock, making the pulse width directly linked to simulation accuracy. Therefore, any adjustment to the duration must be carefully evaluated against the clock period and the FSM's timing requirements to avoid simulation errors or unintended behavior.
