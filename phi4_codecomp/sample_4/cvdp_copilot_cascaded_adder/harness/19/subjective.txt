In the cascaded adder design, the first stage simply passes the input data without significant combinational logic, so keeping it combinational (as in REG = 8'b11111110) avoids the extra clock cycle delay that a register would introduce. The subsequent stages are registered to break up the critical path, ensuring that the overall delay is governed by the pipelined stages rather than the first stage. Therefore, using REG = 8'b11111110 minimizes the initial latency, reducing the total time to produce a valid output compared to REG = 8'b11111111, where an unnecessary register at the first stage adds extra delay.
