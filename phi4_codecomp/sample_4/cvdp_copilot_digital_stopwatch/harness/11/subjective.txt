When the start_stop signal is de-asserted, the first always block stops incrementing the clock divider counter and forces the one_sec_pulse signal to 0, effectively freezing the pulse generation. Consequently, the one_sec_pulse signal never triggers the stopwatch logic, so the seconds, minutes, and hour counters do not update. Additionally, the running flag is cleared (set to 0) in the second always block, confirming that the stopwatch is paused. Therefore, the module internally halts time counting until the start_stop signal is reasserted, at which point the clock divider resumes its operation and eventually generates a one_sec_pulse to update the counters.
