The testbench simulates the VGA controller by applying a 25 MHz clock and a reset signal, and then uses counters to track horizontal pixel cycles and vertical line cycles. It verifies the horizontal synchronization by ensuring that after 640 active pixels, the design transitions through a front porch of 16 clocks, a sync pulse of 96 clocks (where hsync is low), and a back porch of 48 clocks, and similarly confirms the vertical synchronization by validating 480 active lines followed by a front porch of 10 lines, a sync pulse of 2 lines (with vsync low), and a back porch of 33 lines. Throughout the simulation, the testbench displays key messages at each transition, thereby confirming that the hsync and vsync signals conform to the 640 x 480 VGA specifications.
