The testbench uses a single clock signal (clk) generated with a fixed 10ns period to ensure that all DUT instances operate synchronously. All DUTs share the same clock, reset, and enable signals, which guarantees that their internal state transitions occur simultaneously on every clock edge. The run_test task explicitly waits for the positive edge of the clock before reading and logging the outputs, thereby preventing timing mismatches during signal monitoring. By synchronizing all instances to a common clock, the testbench ensures that even though each DUT has unique configurations, their outputs are sampled in lockstep. This coordinated approach minimizes the risk of race conditions by ensuring that signal updates occur at predictable times. Consequently, the consistent clocking and controlled monitoring of out_val and done signals across all instances allow for accurate and reliable verification of the DUT behavior.
