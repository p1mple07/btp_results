The mask signal complicates validation for mode 11 because it applies a bitwise AND to the shifted result, making the expected output dependent on both the shift operation and the specific bit pattern of the mask. The testbench verifies correctness by setting mode to 11, applying a predetermined mask and shift direction, and comparing the actual output with a pre-calculated expected value. This validation is critical as it ensures the barrel shifter correctly implements the masked shift functionality, which is essential for reliable selective bit manipulation in digital designs.
