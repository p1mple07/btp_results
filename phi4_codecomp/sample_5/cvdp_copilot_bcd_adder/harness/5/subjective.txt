In the bcd_adder module, the signals z1 and z2 are used to detect when the binary_sum falls into specific ranges that require correction: z1 is set when both the most significant bit and the second most significant bit of binary_sum are 1, indicating a result in the 10–11 range, while z2 is set when the most significant bit and the third most significant bit are 1, indicating a result in the 8–9 range. The binary_cout signal represents the carry-out from the first addition, which occurs when the binary_sum is in the 12–15 range, thereby signaling an overflow beyond the valid BCD range. Together, these three signals determine when the binary result is outside the acceptable BCD range (0–9) and trigger the necessary correction to ensure the final result remains a valid BCD digit.
