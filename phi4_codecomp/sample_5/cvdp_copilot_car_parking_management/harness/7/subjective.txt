The simulate_entry task holds the vehicle_entry_sensor signal high for 10ns to ensure that the input event is captured within a complete clock cycle. In this design, the clock period is 10ns, which means that a 10ns pulse coincides with one full cycle, allowing the state machine to reliably detect the event. The FSM in the DUT relies on the sensor signal being maintained for at least one clock period to transition from the IDLE state to ENTRY_PROCESSING. If the pulse duration were shortened, the signal might not be sampled properly, potentially causing the FSM to miss the event and fail to initiate the correct state transition. Conversely, extending the pulse beyond 10ns could delay the state transition or even cause unintended behavior if the FSM misinterprets the prolonged signal. Therefore, while the duration can be modified, it must be carefully tuned to match the clock period and the timing requirements of the state transitions to ensure accurate simulation results.
