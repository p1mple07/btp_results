If a value less than 6 is chosen for WIDTH, the signals A, B, and C, as well as the DUT's outputs, will be represented using a narrower bit width, causing the larger test input values (like 31 or -15) to be truncated and not reflect their intended magnitudes. Consequently, the computed GCD and the expected result (which is masked using (1 << WIDTH) - 1) will also be reduced, leading to mismatches in the testbench results and potential test failures.
