The testbench simulates the VGA controller by generating a 25 MHz clock, applying a reset, and incrementing cycle and line counters to monitor the transitions between active display, front porch, sync pulse, and back porch intervals. It displays diagnostic messages at the precise boundaries—640 active pixels followed by 16 front porch clocks, 96 sync pulse clocks, and 48 back porch clocks for the horizontal phase, and similarly 480 active lines followed by 10 front porch lines, 2 sync pulse lines, and 33 back porch lines for the vertical phase—to verify the correct timing. This approach confirms that the hsync and vsync signals conform to the 640 x 480 VGA specification by ensuring each phase occurs for its exact required duration.
